Roster & Depth Chart

  • Patriots rookie Quintayvious Hutchins faces tough roster challenge

    Quintayvious Hutchins, a seventh-round pick, has the most difficult path to secure a spot on the New England Patriots' roster for the 2026 season, following a crowded edge rusher position and competition from established players like Harold Landry and Dre'Mont Jones. Hutchins struggled in college, raising questions about his viability as an NFL player, compounded by an assault and battery charge. This makes his chances of making the final 53-man roster quite slim, prompting speculation about his future with the team.

Injuries

  • Patriots edge rusher Harold Landry placed on PUP list

    Harold Landry has been placed on the physically unable to perform (PUP) list, missing spring workouts as he continues rehabilitation from a knee injury sustained in the previous season. His absence raises concerns over the team's edge depth, as rookie Gabe Jacas remains unsigned, leaving New England vulnerable in this position when training camp begins.

  • Christian Gonzalez's extension likely tied to Devon Witherspoon's deal

    New England cornerback Christian Gonzalez continues to seek a contract extension. However, negotiations may be stalled as the Patriots await the outcome of Devon Witherspoon's expected extension, which might set the market for Gonzalez. Both players aim to become the highest-paid at their position, and the delay in signing could negatively impact Gonzalez's training camp participation.
