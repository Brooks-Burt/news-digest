Roster & Depth Chart

  • Second-round rookie Gabe Jacas remains unsigned

    The Patriots face uncertainty with rookie linebacker Gabe Jacas, who has missed spring practices and is not signed as training camp begins. This absence raises concerns over his readiness, especially as the team will rely on him for edge depth in the absence of Harold Landry, who is on the PUP list. If Jacas does not resolve his contract situation soon, he risks a redshirt season, complicating New England's defensive plans and depth chart.

Injuries

  • Harold Landry placed on physically unable to perform list

    Patriots edge rusher Harold Landry will start training camp on the physically unable to perform (PUP) list due to a knee injury that has sidelined him since spring practices. His absence poses challenges for New England's edge rusher depth following the loss of K'Lavon Chaisson and could impact the team's pass rush effectiveness in the early part of the season.

Contracts & Business

  • Christian Gonzalez's contract situation looms over training camp

    Cornerback Christian Gonzalez is in discussions for a contract extension, which has become a significant storyline as training camp commences. With expectations for Gonzalez to become one of the highest-paid corners in the league, his contract situation could affect both his performance and the defensive strategy for the upcoming season, especially if negotiations extend into the regular season.

  • Craig Woodson named a breakout candidate for 2026

    Safety Craig Woodson has been highlighted as a potential breakout player for the Patriots this season after leading all defenders in snaps during his rookie year. His versatility and solid instincts make him an essential part of the New England defense, particularly with the added presence of veteran Kevin Byard, which could allow Woodson to thrive in his second year.
